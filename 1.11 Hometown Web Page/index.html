Plainfield, Indiana

Plainfield is located in central Indiana, just southwest of Indianapolis. It's located right at the edge of Hendricks County.

About

History

Plainfield was founded in 1839 by Elias Hadley and Levi Jessup. Many of the early settlers of Plainfield were Quakers, members of the Religious Society of Friends. The Quakers are still referenced today in the logo/mascot of the local schools. Plainfield was known for an incident in 1842 where, in protest of President Martin Van Buren's veto of a bill aiming to repair highways, a coachman drove Van Buren's carriage fast through a disrepaired section of US 40 that went through the town, causing the president to fall into a pile of mud.

Interesting Places

Plainfield is home to multiple parks, its largest being Hummel Park. It has a recreation center with a waterpark, and it is home to the Vandalia Trail, which stretches for miles into other towns in Hendricks County. Plainfield has been the headquarters of the Islamic Society of North America since the 1980's

Arts

Plainfield is home to Hendricks Live!, a building that hosts stage events like concerts and plays, as well as art galleries.

Things To Do

  • Go to one of the multiple parks.
  • Catch a concert of stage play at Hendricks Live!
  • Eat dinner at the historic Oasis Diner.
  • Enjoy upscale dining at The Prewitt Restaurant + Lounge.
  • Go to Splash Island, the waterpark at the recreation center.
